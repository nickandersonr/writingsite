The Past

I was known for the drawings on the backs of tests, not the scores on the front.

Sometimes this got my extra credit, but usually it just got my mom called into a parent-teacher conference.

It wasn't until a high school creative writing teacher handed me an article on digital art that things started to fall into place - I saw a fresh, new path out of the small town I was in, and enrolled in the Colorado Institute of Art.

Here I saw my true skills tested - I lead the class earning the Par Excellence award for best portfolio and assisted the faculty and staff with internal projects, including designing the school site for Summer Studio 2008. To this day, I still serve on AI's Professional Advisory Committee which aids them in keeping curriculumn current for incoming students, as well as judging the portfolios of students wishing to graduate.

 

Work Completed:

  • Summer Studio 2008
  • Lisa DiMichele
  • Namlo International

Lessons Learned:

As Einstein is frequently misquoted as saying, "if you judge a fish by its ability to climb a tree, it will live its whole life believing that it is stupid." Whoever actually said this was probably a first year art student, finally feeling the extent of their own ability. Though I'm thankful for the many failures that brought me to my life as a designer/developer, I can't say I miss them.

Biggest Accomplishment:

Earned a scholarship that paid 1/3 of my tuition - with an intensly animated PowerPoint. Imagine my elation when I learned After Effects.

Biggest
mistake:

I really wish I would have paid more attention in math. They were right - I really do use it every day . . .

Active Website

A proffessor once told me that I would be a prostitute for art.

But I wasn't ready to admit that. I held onto my art school ideas as I surfed up Active's career ladder - they were a bustling agency that nearly doubled in size durring my two years there, and I rode that wave all the way from junior front end assitant to Lead Marketing Designer. I was given what, in retrospect, was an insane level of agency over our brand and many others - and I used it to create some of my greatest creative work to date.

Unfortunately, I was still very young and very stupid. I managed to end my employment in a drag out fight with a creative director, over decidedly art-school principles. I wanted things my way, the best way, the way it should be - but I had yet to understand that the web industry was one removed from idealism. I took my lofty aspirations home with me in carboard crate, along with my design journals and company awards, and prepared to rise again.

 

Work Completed:

  • Skogman Realty*
  • Waste Management*
  • 2009 Invite Campaign*

Lessons Learned:

Don't be rude to your Creative Director, they can fire you. Seriously though, I learned that my job was to please the client, not myself. This was a tough lesson, but mayb the most important I've learned - you're only as successful as the people you help.

Biggest Accomplishment:

Created some of my greatest ideas and design work thanks to a trusted, hands-off approach by my employers.

Biggest
mistake:

Got fired, thanks to cockiness fueled by said trust and hands-off approach. I wasn't ready for it.

 

Special Skills Developed:

  • Lofty Goals
    Always shot for the moon, even if I burned up in re-entry
  • Perfect Fit
    Filled every role needed - designer, developer, photographer, and more.
  • Crash and Burn
    Conducted extensive boundary testing on self, others

2012

Zerista

My first task at Zerista was to put together my chair.

I was told that in a couple weeks my work will have paid for it, as if that was something I should be thanking them for. Zerista, I quickly discovered, was much different than my last agency.

After a month of working til 11pm or later, I formed callouses. Not on my fingers, or feet, but on my spirit - I saw the deadlines, the stakes, laid out before me like towering mountain peaks. I had no choice, so I climbed them. Each time I thought I would fail, but each time, somehow, I managed to scale it. The reward for this climb was another peak, taller than the last. I learned to love it.

 

Work Completed:

  • BioPharm Conference*
  • Sonic ConeyConnect*
  • FameCon 2012*

Lessons Learned:

The internet is hard. Startups are hard. Walking out of this job, I understood the fragile floor on which most development is built - it changes daily, it needs constant effort, and often times you have to build your own solution. Not only did I learn to trust in myself and create my own success, I learned not to whine when I had to.

Biggest Accomplishment:

Redesigned and created the company's site all by myself. It was my frist time leading a project in both design AND implementation.

Biggest
mistake:

Took down an entire pharma convention in China for three hours. Learned valuable lesson on source control.

 

Special Skills Developed:

  • Going Up
    Learned to see obstacles as a challenge
  • Panic Mode
    Learned to embrace short deadlines
  • Mechanic
    Became fast and efficient at basic tasks
